How to measure anything

Posted in business books - Sunday, April 29, 2018

Summary of key points from the book How To Measure Anything by Douglas W. Hubbard.

There are a number of reasons why a person might think something can’t be measured. However, in reality these reasons reveal 1 of 3 misconceptions about a particular aspect of measurement:

  1. Concept of measurement: The definition of measurement itself is misunderstood.
  2. Object of measurement: the object of measurement is not well defined.
  3. Methods of measurement: an existing empirical observational method is not known to the person doing the measurement.

In addition, there are 3 common reasons used to suggest why something shouldn’t be measure:

  1. An economic objection: it would be too expensive to carry out the measurement.
  2. An objection to the usefulness of statistics: typically a statement that you can prove anything with statistics so it’s not worth carrying out the measurement..
  3. An ethical objection: it would be immoral to measure something.

Of these 3 objections, the first is the only one with merit but it’s used too often.
